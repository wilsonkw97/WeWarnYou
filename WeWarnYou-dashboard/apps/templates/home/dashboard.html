Line Graph

Flood tweets against timeline

Pulling tweets from Twitter on the duration of 2 weeks since 25th December last year, we can see a spike during Christmas. The second wave of flood hit during that period of time, causing more users to post updates. MOF Malaysia also announced the allowance of RM 1,000 per household on that week and Twitter users are venting their frustrations on the aid provided by our government. Users are also pinpointing on how the government has come to aid in comparison to how other individuals and the opposition has come to aid immediately on the field. Another gradual spike in the first week of this year is because the floods started to worsen once again with heavy rains over several states like Pahang, Johor, Melaka and Kelantan.

Word Cloud

Most Mentioned Location

From the location analysis, we can see Shah Alam frequently mentioned in tweets by users because the flood has heavily hit this city in the first wave of floods especially at Taman Sri Muda. Next in line is Segamat in Johor. The data restriction only allows us to deal more with the recent flood situation and thus, Segamat is the worst hit city in the past two weeks. Klang is not far away because in the overall flood situation since December 2021, Klang can be said to be the worst city in the country to be hit by the floods. Kuantan has also taken its place amongst Twitter users given the bad floods hitting them earlier this year.

Word Cloud

Most Mentioned Keyword

From the word cloud, we see the frequently mentioned words mostly relate to a single topic, which is to share the help needed for the flood victims via Twitter and get volunteers and donations for items. The frustrations and update of situations is also equally much, but however most of the data is via images and will require image analysis for a better understanding on how we can actually have a better system to alert the people. The hashtag #rakyatjagarakyat and #benderaputih becomes trending once again during this flood situation, showing how united the people is during times in need.

Pie Chart

Political Party Mentioned in Tweets

Over here, we provide a brief political analysis on which parties frequetly sparks discussion amongst Twitter users, and as expected, mostly from our government due to frustrations or disappointment with what is being mentioned by our fellow politicians. PAS holds a big weight because of their comments on the flood situation being a religious issue and sheds responsibilities. Looking over to the blue side of the chart showing our ruling party, Barisan Nasional, mostly sparking discussions on how they only offer monetary aid, and doing their part on public relations on social medias, giving a fake impression. Lastly, we have our opposition party for instance DAP, PKR and not forgetting MUDA which came to aid of the flood victims as soon as possible in the early stages of the flood crisis.
